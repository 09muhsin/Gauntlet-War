The trigger isn't an ad — it's the moment she laces up, feels that twinge, and thinks *new shoes, the good ones this time.* Then she does what she always does: opens a tab, types "best running shoes for knee pain," and reads. ## Recommended Approach

Four channels. Each one earns its slice. No vanity spend.

### SEO — $3,500
Own the searches she's already typing. "Running shoes for knee pain." "Cushioned shoe under $150." We build pages that answer the question instead of dressing up a product blurb. These don't pop overnight — they start trickling in week two and never stop. Still selling shoes in month six.

### Long-form Content — $4,000
One signature piece her running coach forwards to the team. Not "5 Tips for Runners." Something with a spine: *"We took this shoe to mile 18 of a marathon. Here's exactly what fell apart on the other guys — and what didn't."* Real testing, real photos, real numbers. This is what makes the SEO traffic *buy* instead of bounce.

### Email Nurture — $3,000
Catch the week-long deciders. A pop-up dangling the comparison guide: *"4 things to check before you spend $120 on running shoes — most people skip #3."* Then a short sequence — the guide, why this shoe exists, one runner's mile-by-mile take, and a launch-day offer with a 30-day run-it-and-return-it guarantee. The guarantee kills the last flinch before checkout.

### PR Outreach — $4,500
Get into the running newsletters and gear-review sites her group already trusts. Not a blast — five to ten pitches to writers who actually log miles in the shoes they review, plus pairs seeded to a few credible voices for honest takes. One "best new shoe of the year" line outpulls 50,000 ad impressions for a $120 product.

**Total: $15,000. Exactly the ceiling.**

## The Funnel Math

The real path to 1,000:

- Combined reach across SEO, content, PR, and the email-driving content: **~250,000 people see us** in 30 days.
- **~5% click through** → **12,500 visitors.**
- **~25% hand over their email** for the comparison guide → **3,125 subscribers.**
- The sequence + guarantee converts **~30%** of engaged subscribers over the window.
- Direct-from-content and PR-driven sales layer on top.

Lands at **1,000–1,050 orders.** The 30-day guarantee is what lifts the conversion rate — it removes the "what if they don't fit" fear that kills the $120 cart.

At $120 a pair, 1,000 orders = **$120,000 in first-month revenue on $15,000 spent.** An 8x return — and the SEO and email list keep paying after the 30 days close.

## The Risks

Not bulletproof. Three real ones:

**1. SEO is slow.** It may not fully land inside 30 days. *Fallback:* content and email carry the window; SEO is the bonus that pays out in month two. We're not betting the goal on it.

**2. PR is a maybe.** Writers don't owe us coverage. If three of ten pitches go cold, visibility dips. *Fallback:* we keep **$1,500 of the PR line flexible** and shift it into seeding more pairs and a small paid boost on the best-performing content piece.

**3. No list yet.** We're building from zero in real time. If capture lags, the day-28 wave is thin. *Fallback:* front-load the comparison-guide pop-up everywhere, lean on the content piece for sign-ups in weeks 1–2, and watch the subscriber count daily. If it's behind by day 10, we sweeten the offer.

Each one has a lever. None of them sinks the plan.
