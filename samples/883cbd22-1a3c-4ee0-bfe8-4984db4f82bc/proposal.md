## Recommended Approach

**One Big Idea ties it all together: "You've been graded on the wrong test."** Every post, every Reel, every influencer brief ladders up to that one reframe. Not "career coaching." Not "multiple intelligence." The wrong test. That's the hook we own.

### TikTok + Instagram Reels — the reach engine
Raw, face-to-camera. The format: someone names the exact moment they knew. *"I closed three deals that quarter and still cried in my car. Turns out I'm wired to build things, not sell them. The 45-minute test told me what 11 years of work never did."* Show the result on screen. The old job, the new one. The face when it clicks. We chase 1 million organic views. One break-out clip carries a month.

### LinkedIn — where the buyers actually are
Same idea, grown-up clothes. Carousels and longer text confessions for the people who can expense coaching and won't admit it out loud. We aim at exactly the seniority that feels trapped. Lower volume, highest intent.

### Micro-influencer partnerships — borrowed trust
We don't buy reach. We hand career-niche creators the test for free and film them reacting to their own results. Real surprise on a real face beats anything we'd script. Tightest niche wins, not the biggest follower count.

### Email — the catcher's mitt
A 45-minute assessment is a commitment. Most won't say yes on the first view. So we grab the email with a free mini-version, then send the stories the algorithm won't show everyone. This is where the slow-burn buyers convert at day 40 instead of never.

### Community (Discord / Facebook) — the durability play
A room for people mid-pivot, post-test. This is the 8 on durability. It keeps people warm, makes the next round of real stories for us, and turns one client into three referrals. It compounds while we sleep.

## The Risks

I'm not going to pretend these away.

**1. The algorithm doesn't cooperate.** Organic reach is rented, not owned. Under 1M views and the whole funnel shrinks. *Fallback:* we shoot volume from week one and kill what flops fast. We don't need every clip — we need one or two. LinkedIn's 5,000 visits never depend on going viral, so we never hit zero.

**2. Influencer content falls flat.** Borrowed trust dies if the creator's audience isn't our stuck-at-work pro. *Fallback:* niche fit over follower count, test small before we commit to anyone.

**3. The 45 minutes scares people off.** It's the real constraint. *Fallback:* the free mini-version grabs the email, lowers the ask, and the email sequence sells the full test once trust is built.

And the honest one: **400 isn't 1,000.** I'd rather say that now than explain it in month four. 400 is what $0 buys in 90 days. The road to 1,000 runs through the list and the community we build getting there.
